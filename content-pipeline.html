The Problem

You recorded great stuff.
Now comes the grind.

Every Sunday, the same scene.

A creator sits down in front of a week's worth of recorded video, knowing that each hour of raw footage needs to become twelve, fifteen, maybe thirty separate pieces of content. LinkedIn posts. Tweets. Newsletter. Carousels. YouTube shorts. Each platform, its own format.

By Wednesday, the insights are stale. The algorithm has moved on. The content never ships.

Not because the creator is lazy. Because the math doesn't work: recording one hour costs one hour. Repurposing it takes six. Multiply that by four videos a month, and you've got a second job you didn't sign up for.

03
The Craft

We didn't build a generator.
We built her voice at scale.

The instinct when automating content is to use AI to write faster. That's wrong. The instinct should be to use AI to never lose your voice — even at 10× output.

01

Your voice is the product

Claude was trained on 200+ of her past posts to learn her cadence, her openings, her favorite phrases. Every generated piece matches her rhythm — not a generic "AI voice".

02

Craft, then scale

The pipeline generates eleven formats in parallel from each video — but they're not variants of each other. Each one is written in its platform's native style.

03

You remain the editor

Nothing publishes automatically. Everything lands in Notion for review. Edit 20%, delete 10%, publish 70%. Your brand, your rhythm, your filter.

04

Learn every week

Analytics flow back: which formats win, which openings convert, which topics resonate. The system gets smarter with each cycle.

The Process

Fourteen days. Six phases.
Zero scope creep.

Days1—2

Voice deep-dive

Imported 200+ of her past LinkedIn posts, tweets, and newsletters. Analyzed patterns in rhythm, vocabulary, and structure. Built the voice signature.

Kickoff call (1h) Voice corpus indexed Tone analysis report
Days3—4

Format specs & visual templates

Defined the 11 output formats with her. Built Canva-style templates matching her visual brand. Milestone 1 paid (30%) at spec sign-off.

11 format specs Visual templates (3 variants) Milestone 1 paid
Days5—8

Pipeline assembly

Deployed n8n + Whisper + Claude stack on Railway. Wired the 18-node workflow. First generation test yielded 72% tone match. Iteration began.

Days9—11
